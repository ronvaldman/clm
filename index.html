    1. What is CLM?

    CLM — the Continuous Learning Model — is a new way to think about learning. It’s a framework where knowledge isn’t fixed but evolves continuously through open collaboration, community review, and shared discovery.

    Inspired by both academic publishing and the open-source movement, CLM treats every page, lesson, and idea as part of a living system that grows with its contributors — not a static textbook, but a versioned knowledge ecosystem.

    In practice:

    • Content is versioned and openly editable.
    • Every contribution is transparent, credited, and traceable.
    • Learners are encouraged to participate as co-authors, not just readers.

    1.1 Why CLM Exists

    GitHub lets us collaborate on code.
    CLM extends that spirit to knowledge itself.

    Most learning platforms stop at consumption — read, watch, complete.
    CLM continues into co-creation: you learn by contributing to the very material you study.

    It’s not about replacing universities or GitHub — it’s about creating a space where education feels open-source, peer-driven, and alive.

    2. Who Is It For?

    CLM is designed for anyone who wants to learn, teach, or build openly — without institutional walls.

    • Students who want structured, peer-reviewed study materials.
    • Educators who wish to publish open textbooks that improve through versioned community input.
    • Developers and researchers who enjoy treating knowledge like open-source code — branch, improve, merge.

    3. How It Works

    1. Fork or clone the repo.
    2. Add or improve anything.
    3. Submit a pull request for peer review.
    4. Approved changes merge into the main CLM edition.

    Each page evolves over time — continuously.

    4. Contributing and Licensing

    CLM is released under a Creative Commons BY-SA license.
    Contributors retain credit while allowing anyone to build upon their work — with attribution.

    See How to Contribute for full guidelines.

    5. Roadmap

    CLM is built openly, with each phase shaped by community feedback and contribution.

    Upcoming milestones:

    • Public launch of the CLM platform at learn-clm.com
    • Core Computer Science curriculum covering foundations, programming, systems, and theory
    • Peer-review workflow to enable collaborative editing and knowledge validation
    • Contributor tools for authors and educators to publish their own modules
    • Expansion beyond CS into new disciplines under the Continuous Learning Model
